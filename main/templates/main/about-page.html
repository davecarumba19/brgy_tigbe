Our History

In the year 1730, Tigbe was a barrio with many people living in it but it was under the town of Angat. In this place many trees called “tigbe” could be found all around mostly grown along the sides of brooks and hills. Hence, the early people in this place called barrio Tigbe. Not long after the year 1730, Norzagaray became independent of Angat from the proclamation of the archbishop of Manila. The proclamation was on August 1860. In this proclamation. Tigbe too was separated from Angay, thus become a barrio of Norzagaray. Since then many people from nearby places like bigan, Angat, Sta. Maria and San Jose Del Monte plucked into place, built their houses and made the place as their home. Their houses were built of light materials available in the barrio like cogon and bamboo. Some houses have woods as their posts. During that time there was already a road within the barrio. The houses were built along the road. The early settlers of the barrio worked hard to strive for better means of livelihood. They cleared the forest, made their kaingins, worked in the fields and raised animals. They later formed the place a nice one to live in. they enjoyed living in the place until at last they realized their progress. At that time, they brought their products to Bocawe, Bulacan wherein they exchanged them with their everyday needs like clothing, salt and other crops or food that could not be raised in the barrio.

        During that time, the people had also their head, a lieutenant who took responsible of the people’s welfare. Apolinario Bayonbon was then their barrio lieutenant. He served as the consultant of the people whenever any trouble aroused. He safeguards the barrio very well. The people had also their religion. They believed in Christianity and so they are binded buy this religion. Through the iniative of Apolinario Bayonbon, the people were able to build their chapel wherein masses were held when they hold their feast and any other religious celebrations. In this chapel, many people learned their alphabet too, under the teaching instruction of the teacher known as Maestrong Bining who came from Angat, Bulacan, but he was deprived of processing a longer life so he died after a short period of teaching the people. The Latter replaced his position and so more people became literate. The people admired Maestrang Iska known as “Fracisca alias Turmok” because she was a genius. The people upon learning to read and write were elevated into a higher way of life. They lived cooperatively and harmoniously with esach other. Regarding social condition the people are not behind. They also celebrated parties, such as wedding, baptismal, and birthday parties.
